Tesla's ability to scale production depends heavily on its "Gigafactories." The first one, Giga Nevada, produces electric motors and battery packs. Giga New York focuses on Solar Roof and electrical components. 

The international expansion started with Giga Shanghai, which was built in record time and now serves as Tesla's primary export hub. Giga Berlin-Brandenburg was Tesla's first manufacturing location in Europe, focusing on the Model Y. Giga Texas, the global headquarters, is where the Cybertruck and Model Y are produced using the latest "structural battery pack" technology. 

Tesla's engineering approach involves "unboxed" manufacturing processes and massive "Giga Press" casting machines to reduce the number of parts in a car. This efficiency allows Tesla to maintain higher profit margins than traditional automakers like Ford or GM.
