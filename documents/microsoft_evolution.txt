Microsoft's rise to dominance started with MS-DOS in the early 1980s, followed by the massive success of Microsoft Windows. The launch of Windows 95 was a pivotal moment in computing history, introducing the Start menu and taskbar that remain central to the UI today. 

Under the leadership of Satya Nadella, who became CEO in 2014, Microsoft shifted its focus from "Windows-first" to "Cloud-first, Mobile-first." This led to the rapid growth of Azure, which now competes directly with Amazon Web Services (AWS). Microsoft's acquisition strategy has been aggressive, buying LinkedIn for $26 billion in 2016, GitHub in 2018, and Activision Blizzard in 2023 for $68.7 billion. 

Currently, Microsoft is deeply integrated with OpenAI, using GPT technology to power "Copilot" across its software suite. This integration has turned Microsoft into an AI powerhouse, providing enterprise-grade AI solutions to millions of businesses worldwide.
